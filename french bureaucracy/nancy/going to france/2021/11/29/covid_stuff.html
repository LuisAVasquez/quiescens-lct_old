Even though France is one of the countries where the antivax movement was the strongest, their vaccination campaign against COVID is widespread and covers foreigners without many hussles.

Update: This post has been updated with information up to May 2022, when COVID restrictions have been almost fully lifted in the whole of France.

Testing

When I arrived to Nancy (September 2022), antigene testing was available in most pharmacies. Now, as the months have passed, it is only available in the most centric ones.

If you have a Carte Vitale, you can get antigen tested for free, otherwise, you have to pay 25€.

The results are available through a website (they'll tell you which one there) in at most one hour, and from there you can download a certificate of a negative result.

To get a PCR test, one has to go to a laboratory. I got one at "Atoutbio", close to the Gare.

Getting vaccinated

I arrived to Nancy without being vaccinated yet, not due to lack of will but due to delays in my country. Anyway, the good news is that you don't need to be registered in the French healtcare system to get vaccinated!

It is enough to book an appointment at a vaccination center through Doctolib (you may need a French phone number though!), where you can even select which vaccine you'd prefer, if it is available (I got Pfizer).

The first dose will be the most complicated to get. Take your passport to the appointment, you will be asked for your Carte Vitale, but it is enough to tell them that you do not have it yet. In my case, they called in a supervisor to sort out my situation: They gave me some papers stating that I was being registered for vaccination at that center. It is very important to take these papers to get the next doses.

Then I went in for vaccination, showed the doctors the papers I had just got, and was ready to head out after 30 minutes.

Note: Just before leaving the vaccination center, there are some people in charge of generating the certificate of vaccination. It is better if you talk to them and ask them to email it to you or print it there. Why? Because normally this certificate would be available through the website of the French healthcare system, and since you're not registered yet, you will not get it on that website. If you forget to get it, you have to go back to the vaccination center and ask for it. This vaccination certificate has a QR you can use to get your Passe Sanitaire.

The second and third doses need basically the same procedure. Each dose generates a new certificate of vaccination.

Passe Sanitaire

The Passe Sanitaire was a proof of vaccination required to enter some public places. France implemented it through the app TousAntiCovid (all against COVID), available for Android and iOS.

To activate the Passe Sanitaire, there are two options:

  • A temporary activation: This is done with a certificate of a negative result for a test.

  • A permanent activation: This is done with a vaccination certificate.

Both of these certificates contain a QR code which can be scanned to get a Passe Sanitaire. The application will generate a QR which can be used as proof of vaccination.

The QRs from this application also work abroad! I have used the permanent Passe Sanitaire as proof of vaccination when travelling to other countries in Europe!

Foreign vaccinations

What if you have been already vaccinated abroad?

If you have been vaccinated with one of the vaccines recognized by the European Union, you just have to take your proof of vaccination to any pharmacy. There they will generate a French vaccination certificate for you. I have not done this myself, this is what I have heard from other students.

If you have been vaccinated with one of the vaccines not recognized by the European Union (like the Russian Sputnik 5), you will have to get vaccinated in France.
