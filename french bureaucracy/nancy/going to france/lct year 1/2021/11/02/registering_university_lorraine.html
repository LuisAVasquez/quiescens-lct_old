Having the status of a French student gives you access to many perks, like student discounts, the right to eat at the student cafeteria, access to the online platforms of the UniversitÃ© de Lorraine, etc.

Students accepted for the local Master's at the IDMC are automatically registered as French students, LCT students must do it in person.

Getting an appointment

You should ask the LCT Coordinator for the email of the Administrative Coordinator of the IDMC. Then, simply write them an email asking for an appointment. The current Administrative Coordinator does not speak English really well, but they can manage to help you.

I recommend you do the registration just a couple of days after arrival, and even better, before the classes start: during the semester, the Administrative Coordinator gets really busy.

Necessary documents

The following documents will be necessary for the registration:

  • Your Passport (with a copy)
  • A form given by the LCT Coordinator: this is a form from the University of Lorraine. Fill your personal information and the Administrative Coordinator will help you fill the rest.
  • A health insurance certificate. I just used the one I got after being accepted to the LCT program.
  • Optionally, the CVEC excemption certificate, but this document is not really necessary.
  • A high school diploma (or something that certifies that you finished high school), and a copy. Preferably translated to English or French.
  • An ID photo for your student card.

With all of this, the appointment took me around 20 minutes. The Administrative Coordinator told me that I would receive an email with details for further steps.

Where to get an ID photo?

If you don't have ID photos available, you can go into photo booths around the city. There are some inside the Gare and some malls. They also work in English.

Entering the IDMC

On the day of your appointment, you will have to enter the IDMC. However, for students, it is only accessible through a student card. To enter, just ring the bell of the reception, say you have an appointment with the Administrative Coordinator, and they will let you in.

After the registration

University Account activation

A couple of days after the registration, the Administrative Coordinator will send you an email with details for activating your student account and your email.

Student card

During the first or second week of class, you will receive your student card. After getting it, you should go to the reception of your CROUS residence, and tell them to activate your card so that it can open the main door.

Academic registration

During the first month of class, the Administrative Coordinator will send you an email saying you have to do the "Academic registration". This just means that, now that you are registered as a student, you have to register the list of courses that you will take for the academic year.

The Academic registration is done online with a link provided by the Administrative Coordinator, and it is pretty easy to do.
